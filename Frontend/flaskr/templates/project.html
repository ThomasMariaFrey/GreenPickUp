About

The scope of the Smart Route Planning Tool for Green Parcel Delivery Project is to develop and implement a smart parcel delivery system by using mobile pick-up stations. Customers will be able to pick up their parcels at places they are already at such as their places of work, supermarkets or cultural facilities. This yields a decrease in avoidable traffic, congestion and emissions. We decided to introduce a Reinforcement Learning agent which places mobile pick-up stations at the best possible locations by various parameters. Additionally, we implemented a route planning algorithm to obtain optimal routes between pick-up locations. More information can be found here at the Institute for Enterprise Systems at the University of Mannheim.

The project consists of several software artefacts: First, we created synthetic data by simulating commuter routes through the project city of Stuttgart in Germany. These data is merged with several other features which all play an important role in assessing places throughout the city whether they are a good choice for a mobile pick-up station. Second, a smart agent makes decisions about where to place the pick-up stations throughout the day. Third, a route planning tool picks the best routes between the places determined by the smart agent. Fourth, a frontend application displays the placement decisions and the routes and integrates a database application for parcel delivery.

Project Timeline

The project was taking place from February until August 2023 and we worked mostly remotely on this project. During the project, we had several milestones including literature research, creating a prototype, bringing all the components together and finally finetuning. In March 2023, Mannheim students went on a visit to Cluj for a week and in July 2023, Cluj students went on a re-visit to Mannheim.
